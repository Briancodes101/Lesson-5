Neutralization

Neutralization refers to a chemical reaction in which an acid and a base react to form water and a salt, resulting in a solution with a more neutral pH (closer to 7).

Types of Neutralization

  • Strong Acid + Strong Base: Produces a neutral solution
  • Weak Acid + Strong Base: Produces a slightly basic solution
  • Strong Acid + Weak Base: Results in a slightly acidic solution
  • Weak Acid + Weak Base: Produces a solution that may vary in pH depending on the acid and base strengths.
